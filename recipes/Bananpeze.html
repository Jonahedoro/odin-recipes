Haitian recipes : Banan peze

Description

Banan, or I should say plantains, are quite common in the Caribbean.Peze means press. So essentially, banan peze is "pressed plantains". If fried the right way, they can be a delicious, crisp appetizer.

This is also a common dish in Haiti.

Ingredients

  • Plantain(s)
  • 2 cups Vegetable Oil
  • 2 tsps. Lime OR Lemon Juice
  • ¾ tsp Salt
  • ½ cup Water

Steps

  1. Get your oil prepared and heated, the oil must be high enough to fully submerge the plantains while frying.
  2. Cut the ends and peel the plantains.
  3. After peeling the plantains, cut them diagonally making about 5 - 6 pieces per plantain.
  4. Once the oil has reached temperature at medium heat, add in a few of the plantain pieces being sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for 4 - 6 minutes.
  5. While the plantains fry, prepare the salt water solution. In a bowl, add 1/2 cup of water, 3/4 tsp. salt, and 2 tsp. lime or lemon juice.
  6. After plantains have fried, remove them from the oil and place on a paper towel lined plate. Using a plantain presser, or the smooth underside of a bowl, flatten each piece of plantain.
  7. The plantains will be fried a second time. However, the temperature of the oil must be increased to HIGH heat. Dip each individual piece of plantain into the salt water mixture for about a second or two and place directly into the hot oil.
  8. Once fried, remove from hot oil and place on paper towel lined plate to absorb any excess oil.
  9. Serve immediately
